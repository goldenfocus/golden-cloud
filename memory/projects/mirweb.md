mirweb's actual mission (per JR, 2026-04-30): build a corp his younger brothers can run so they don't have to work at McDonald's in the summer. Pitch to brothers: grind one year, then have a $50K/year base of recurring MRR that pays while they sleep.

**Why:** family economic mobility for the younger siblings; intergenerational autonomy ladder, not venture-scale, not even "lifestyle SaaS." Hostinger Horizons launched the same surface (AI-generated SMB sites) at $1B scale on 2026-04-30 — does not threaten this mission because the goal isn't market share, it's family payroll.

**How to apply:**
- Hostinger / global SaaS competitors are not the threat. Don't optimize against them or invoke them when discussing strategy.
- Product complexity must stay capped: what JR ships, brothers must operate without him. Every feature is a liability for solo operation.
- Sales kit (tablet demo + 1-page contract + walk-in script + first-week onboarding checklist) is higher-leverage than landing-page polish.
- Verticals must be walk-in-friendly: massage, hair, nails, dépanneurs, restos, barbershops. No B2B SaaS, no gatekeepers, no procurement.
- Geography locked to Montréal-Quebec. French-first.
- Brothers paid as commissioned co-owners (per-signed-customer flat + % of retained MRR), not salaried.
- "Passive income" framing is half-fiction; it's recurring-after-active-sales-grind. Be honest with brothers about the work.
- TAM ceiling at 200 customers × $99 = ~$237K/year is the real target shape, not $10M ARR.
- Don't propose "pivot mirweb" as a strategy in this codebase. The mission predates and overrides any market analysis.
